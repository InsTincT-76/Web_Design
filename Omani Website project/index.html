Where is Oman

At the southeastern edge of the Arabian Peninsula, Oman borders the vast Arabian Sea and Gulf of Oman. The northern region of this country lies at the Musandam Peninsula. Here, it borders the Strait of -Hormuz, which is also near the Persian Gulf. Oman's geographical neighbors include Saudi Arabia, the United Arab Emirates, and Yemen. The borders of Oman's seas are shared with Pakistan and Iran.




Language

The Omani people speak the common Middle Eastern language of arabic. Modern Standard Arabic, the most common version of this dialect, is officially taught in Oman's schools. However, many different colloquial versions of Arabic is spoken, commonly shared with those who are also near the Persian Gulf area. These types of vernacular Arabic are not comprehensible to other regions. For example, those of jibālī culture cannot speak southern arabic because regions such as those are derived from arabic of the North. In addition to Modern Standard Arabic, the Omani can speak English, Persian, and Urdu. Many can also speak Swahili, most often those born in Zanzibar and those who immigrated to Oman from Eastern Africa during a 1970's period of immigration.

Religion

In Oman, people overwhelmingly following the Muslim relgion and culture. The Ibāḍī branch of the Islam, part of the Muslim religion, is a moderate Khārijite group. This group is an an ancient tribe that once populated Oman, and left behind it's teachings. Ibāḍism has very similar beliefs, rituals, and rules as the Sunni Islam branch. The difference between these two branches is the imam. The imam is a spiritual leader of the Ibāḍī people. Those who do not follow Ibāḍism, including those called the Baloch, are often Sunnis. People who are in the south asian region of Oman are mostly Shi'i muslims. However, a few people practice Hinduism.
