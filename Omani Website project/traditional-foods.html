Dates



Palm trees are the most frequently seen plantations around Oman. They produce mouth-watering dates. Differing in size, color, and tenderness, these dates are one of the essential meal items that are presented to every guest as part of the wonderful Omani hospitality. Sometimes, Omanis mix dried dates with sesame paste and sesame or ground coconut. They also use them in preparing different types of desserts and Omani sweets.


Kahwa



Coffee, otherwise known as Kahwa to the Omani, is very popular. Omani coffee is mixed with cardamon powder, usually used to greet guests of Oman. It can also be used when greeting neighbors. This drink is often served with a dessert, otherwise known as Halwa.


Halwa



Halwa means dessert in arabic. The dessert is a thick, brown-colored sweet consisting of sugar, honey, rose water, eegs, spices, and nuts. It is often served with Kahwa and dates. The custom with Halwa is to serve it with Kahwa and dates, which people share with spoons. This tradition brings people closer together.


Harees



Another traditional dish served by the Omani is the harees. This food is made from wheat and meat, served with a traditional sauce.


Majboos



Majboos, also known as kabsa and makboos, is a dish that originated in Saudi Arabia. The dish includes basmati rice, veggies, meat or chicken, and several spices. It can often be served with yogurt or salad, or bread and tomato sauce.


Mashuai



Another popular dish, straight from the seas, is called the mashuai. The mashuai is a plate consisting of grilled kingfish and lemon sauce, covered in Omani spices.


Meshkak



Many are familiar the kebab, food served on wooden sticks. Oman partakes in kebabs by cooking beef, mutton or chicken. It is marinate in spices, curry, and served with traditional bread. It is very common to spy this dish during celebrations.


Shuwa



Shuwa is a very delicious meal served by the Omani. Meaning grilled meat in arabic, this food is prepared for very special occasions, such as the Muslim celebration of Eid. The preparation of this meal takes up to several days to make. It is marinated in spices, wrapped in banana or palm leaves, then buried under the ground. It is then cooked underground. Despite this unusual method of preparation, it does nothing to prevent this dish from being one of the most delicious in all of Oman.
