Few extra information about oman

It's clear that Oman's historical forts, long with-standing time, are a must-see for tourists. Those who may not enjoy history may ask, 'What else does this country have to offer?'. If one were to travel to the outer edges of this land, a long stretch of sand and waves are to be found, coming from the arrabian sea. Traveling inland, tourists can immerse themselves in Omani culture and traditional events. These events can be found on our other page.

On July 23rd, 1970, a historical event took place. Sultan Qaboos Bin Said became Oman's official ruler. Since then, the Omani people have found improvements within their education, economy, and political climate. Oman is rapdidly into a country where people are more educated and financially stable. Oman is a Sultanate, or country where Sultan's pass down their crown to future heirs.

Oman is considered one of the safest countries in the Arab world in refernce to the Global Terrorism Index. As of a report in the late 2010's, Oman had a score of 0, meaning there was little to no terrorist concerns within the communities or government.

The northern, southern, and eastern borders of Oman are surrounded by the sea. Due to this, it appears to be similar to a penninsula. Because Oman is a sea-faring country, one of it's main resources is shipbuilding. This became a main source of revenue. Due to this long-time economic income, Omanis learned how to perfect their craft, becoming one of the top shipbuilding countries in the world.
